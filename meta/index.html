From a psychologist who couldn't move

I started at thirty.
As a psychologist.
With no background.

I'm a clinical psychologist. For two years I studied the mind, telling myself movement wasn't "serious" enough for me.

Then one day I had to move freely in front of a group — and I froze.

I'd spent two years hiding from my own body. And ten years before that telling myself it was too late.

I thank God for the simple steps someone showed me. They didn't fix me — they unlocked me.

Fifteen years of practice later, I've taught people from 15 countries. Most of them told themselves the same thing I did: I'm too late. I'm not the type. My body can't do this.

It was never true. Not for me. Not for them.
