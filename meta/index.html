Most of us treat the body like a machine — something to fix, shape, or improve.

But the way you move shapes how you think, how you feel, how you meet the world. Most people never find out how much.

A note from Roman

I'm a clinical psychologist. For two years I thought movement practice wasn't serious enough for me — I was busy studying the mind.

Then one day I had to move freely in front of a group, and I froze.

I'd spent two years hiding from my own body.

I'd also spent ten years before that telling myself it was too late to start — that I'd look like a fool. That belief cost me a decade.

Fifteen years of practice later, the thing I hear most from the people I teach is the same sentence I used to tell myself: I'm too late. I'm not the type. My body can't do this.

It was never true. Not for me. Not for them. Not for you.
